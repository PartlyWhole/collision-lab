LESSON first — what even is a shape?
A1 MEET THE RECTANGLE Rect(x, y, w, h)

A pygame rectangle is just four numbers: x, y — the top-left corner — plus width and height. Those four define it. Every other member you'll ever use (right, bottom, center…) pygame just computes from them. Drag the box to change x, y — drag the corner ◰ to change width, height.

A2 MEET THE CIRCLE center + radius

A circle is even simpler: a center (x, y) and a radius r. Three numbers, no Rect at all — pygame.draw.circle works straight from center + radius. Drag to move the center; drag the handle to change r.

        01
        RECT vs RECT — AABB
        .colliderect()
      

      

        
The workhorse. Two axis-aligned boxes overlap when their X-ranges overlap and their Y-ranges overlap — four comparisons, no square roots. pygame.Rect bakes it into one call. Cheap enough to run on thousands of objects per frame.

        

          
◢ THE MATH

          
Two boxes touch only when their ranges overlap on both axes at once:

          

            
XA.left < B.right and A.right > B.left

            
YA.top < B.bottom and A.bottom > B.top

          

          
in plain words → if a box is fully left, right, above, or below the other, they miss. If none of those is true, all four checks pass and it's a hit.
